Finding Lost & Found: Designer ’s Notes from the Process of Creating a Jewish Game for Learning

This article provides context for and examines aspects of the design process of a game for learning. Lost & Found (2017a, 2017b) is a tabletop-to-mobile game series designed to teach medieval religious legal systems, beginning with Moses Maimonides’ Mishneh Torah (1180). The aim of this article is to provide insight into a number of the specific decisions faced by the design team in the creation of the first game in the series. Creating games for learning is always a dual-design process, that of creating an engrossing game system for the players while also following the research in games and learning, focusing on enhancing formal and informal learning environments...
